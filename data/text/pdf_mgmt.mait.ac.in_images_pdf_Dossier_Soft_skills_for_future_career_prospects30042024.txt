Proposal:  
  
• Name of the event: Guest lecture on “Soft Skills for Future Career Prospects”  
  
• Date: 30th April, 2024  
  
• Time: 11:00 am – 1:00 pm   
  
• Venue: 741, 7th Block 
 
• Organized By:  Department of Management, MBA, MAIT  
 
  
Objective of the Session:  
  
 
The objective of the guest lecture on the topic “Soft Skills for Future career Prospects are as under 
1. To elucidate why soft skills are crucial for professional success in today's dynamic job 
market. 
2. To identify and define essential soft skills such as communication, teamwork, 
adaptability, problem-solving, leadership, and emotional intelligence. 
3. To provide strategies and techniques for improving soft skills through practical 
exercises, self-assessment tools and real life examples.  
4. To demonstrate how the development of soft skills aligns with long-term career goals 
and enhances employability in various industries.  
5. To explore how individuals can apply soft skills in different professional scenarios, 
including interviews, networking events, and work place interaction. 
 
The overall aim is to provide a comprehensive framework for the guest lecture, is to ensuring that 
participants gain valuable insights and practical tools for enhancing their soft skills and advancing 
their careers.

Aim of the course:  
This training program aims:  
• To highlight the importance of soft skills in professional settings. 
• To acquaint learners that soft skills are crucial for success in the 
dynamic and interconnected world of work. 
  
 
Abstract:  
The session started at 11:00 a.m. in Room no. 741, 7th Block with a brief introduction of the 
topic. The session was conducted by Prof. Jagdish Mehta, who was the former director of 
Doordarshan and is currently working as a visiting professor at GDG University Gurugram 
and Professor of Practice at CPU Hamirpur, Himachal Pradesh.  
First, the students were acquainted with the term Soft Skills. It was elucidated that soft skills 
encompass a broad range of non-technical abilities, including communication, leadership, 
teamwork, adaptability, problem-solving, time management, and emotional intelligence. 
These skills are essential for the effective interaction and collaboration in professional 
settings.  
The speaker underscored the increasing emphasis placed by employers on soft skills during 
the hiring process. In today's dynamic and interconnected world, organizations prioritize 
candidates who not only possess technical competencies but also demonstrate strong 
interpersonal skills. Soft skills are pivotal for fostering a positive work environment, 
enhancing productivity, and driving innovation.  
The guest lecturer highlighted the correlation between proficiency in soft skills and career 
progression. Individuals proficient in communication, for instance, are better equipped to 
convey ideas persuasively, negotiate effectively, and build meaningful relationships with 
colleagues and clients. Moreover, soft skills empower professionals to navigate challenges,

seize opportunities, and adapt to changing circumstances, thereby enhancing their 
employability and promotability  
Practical strategies for honing soft skills were shared during the lecture. The speaker 
emphasized the importance of stepping out of one's comfort zone and embracing opportunities 
for growth and development.  
In conclusion, the guest lecture underscored that while technical expertise may open doors, it 
is soft skills that ultimately determine one's success in the professional arena. Students were 
encouraged to prioritize the cultivation of soft skills alongside their academic pursuits, 
recognizing them as indispensable assets for future career prospects. The session concluded 
with a call to action for individuals to embark on their journey of self-improvement and skill 
enhancement.  
Feedback from attendees was overwhelmingly positive, with many expressing gratitude for 
the insights gained during the lecture. Several participants noted their intention to actively 
work on strengthening their soft skills and integrating them into their career development 
plans.  
 
 
Conclusion:  
The guest lecture served as a catalyst for introspection and action, inspiring individuals to 
invest in their personal and professional growth.   
In total, around 68 students attended the session and many students raised their queries which were 
handled by the speaker very effectively.  Overall, the guest lecture on "Soft Skills for Future Career 
Prospects:” proved to be informative, engaging, impactful and resonating with individuals across 
diverse backgrounds and career stages.
