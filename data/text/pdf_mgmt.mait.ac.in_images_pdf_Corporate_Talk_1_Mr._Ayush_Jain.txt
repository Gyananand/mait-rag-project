Introduction  
The Department of Management (MBA) at Maharaja Agrasen Institute of Technology organized the first 
session of the Corporate Talk Series on February 6, 2025. The session featured Mr. Ayush Jain, Senior 
Vice President at Loan Frame, as the resource person. The session provided valuable insights into the 
essential skills required to get hired, the real interview questions candidates face, and the key attributes 
corporates look for in potential employees. Additionally, the discussion highlighted the significance of 
choosing the right job, one that not only provides employment but also shapes an individual’s career 
trajectory. 
Details of the Session: 
Mr. Ayush Jain started the session by addressing the actual filters that companies use to evaluate 
candidates. He emphasized that beyond academic qualifications, employers prioritize problem-solving 
abilities, adaptability, and a proactive approach to challenges. The key skills that corporates seek include: 
1. Technical Proficiency & Industry Knowledge: Candidates must be well versed in industry trends 
and possess relevant technical expertise. 
2. Critical Thinking & Problem-Solving: Employers look for individuals who can analyse situations 
and provide effective solutions. 
3. Communication & Interpersonal Skills: The ability to convey ideas clearly and work 
collaboratively is a major differentiator. 
4. Emotional Intelligence & Adaptability: Companies value candidates who can handle pressure and 
adapt to changing business environments. 
5. Leadership & Decision-Making: A potential leader who can take initiative is always preferred. 
Mr. Jain also shed light on the real interview questions that candidates frequently encounter, ranging 
from competency-based queries to situational assessments. The session then transitioned into a 
discussion on The Ideal Job.  
Conclusion The Corporate Talk Series 1 provided students with a comprehensive understanding of the 
hiring process, corporate expectations, and strategic job selection. Mr. Ayush Jain’s insights equipped 
students with the knowledge to refine their job search approach, improve their interview performance, 
and make informed career decisions. The session concluded with an engaging Q&A round, where 
students received personalized advice on their career aspirations. 
Overall, the event was highly interactive and beneficial, reinforcing the need for continuous skill 
development and strategic career planning. The Department of Management aims to conduct more such 
sessions in the future to bridge the gap between academia and corporate expectations.
